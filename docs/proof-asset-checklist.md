# Dominion Proof Asset Checklist

Use this after a clean closing or a strong seller interaction.

## Minimum viable proof pack

- Seller first name or initials approved for public use
- City and state approved for public use
- Property type
- Why they reached out
- What condition/problem mattered most
- Why they chose a direct sale instead of listing
- What the timeline looked like
- What made the process feel trustworthy
- One short quote in the seller's actual voice
- One exterior or context photo if approved

## Better case-study pack

- Situation type:
  inherited, landlord, as-is, relocation, foreclosure, divorce, vacant house
- Rough property snapshot:
  age, condition, occupancy, obvious issues
- Main decision pressure:
  repairs, time, distance, family coordination, tenants, uncertainty
- Timeline:
  first call, offer window, title/closing timing
- Process notes:
  title company, flexibility, cleanout, access, timing changes
- Outcome:
  what got easier for the seller

## What good seller quotes sound like

Use plain language. The strongest quotes in this niche usually sound like:

- "We mainly wanted a straight answer."
- "I did not want to put more money into it just to list it."
- "The biggest thing was knowing who we were dealing with."
- "We needed something simple."
- "They explained the process without pushing us."

## What to avoid

- Generic "Great service" quotes
- Perfect marketing-speak
- Fake dollar amounts
- Fake stars or fake badges
- Overexplaining returns or investment language when the seller was thinking emotionally

## Publishing rules

- If the seller did not approve public review use, keep it private.
- If details are blended for privacy, label it as a local deal snapshot rather than a testimonial.
- If a quote is lightly edited for grammar, do not change the meaning or tone.
- Do not imply verification you do not actually have.
